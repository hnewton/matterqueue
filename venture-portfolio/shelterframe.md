## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — Shelterframe

**One Phrase** — *Operational Infrastructure for Human Services* *(uncertain)*

**Core Sentence** — Give temporary and semi-permanent housing operators a shared SaaS infrastructure and open source playbook so every city and nonprofit stops reinventing the same broken wheel and starts delivering consistently compassionate, commercially rigorous human services

**One Paragraph** — Every shelter and transitional-housing operator starts from zero — on intake, policies, service coordination, outcome tracking — reinventing the same broken wheel in every city. Shelterframe gives them shared SaaS infrastructure and an open-source playbook so compassion finally comes with operational discipline, and treats residents as customers with agency rather than passive recipients. A graduation-path mechanic produces the measurable outcomes funders require, and the open policy library means every operator learns from every other. The most complex idea in the set, and one of the most fundable and impactful.

**Key Insights**
- Every shelter and transitional housing operator starts from zero on policies, intake, service coordination, resident communication, and outcome tracking — that institutional waste is the problem
- Commercial operation orientation applied to human services is the unlock — compassion without operational discipline fails the people it's trying to help
- Habitat for Humanity pay-it-forward graduation model creates dignity and agency
- Decentralized and open source design and policy library means every operator benefits from every other operator's learning
- Resident-facing mobile app treats homeless individuals as customers with agency not passive recipients

**User** — Nonprofit housing operators, shelter administrators; city and county housing departments; residents of temporary housing; HUD, state housing authorities, impact investors

**Model** — SaaS licensing to operators; open source policy and design library; grant funding; government contract revenue; nonprofit or social venture structure

**Why It's Real**
- Operational reinvention problem is universal and documentable
- SaaS infrastructure for human services is underbuilt relative to commercial sectors
- Resident-as-customer mobile experience is a reframe that resonates with operators already trying to do this without tools
- Graduation path mechanic creates measurable outcomes that funders require

**Flags** — Most complex idea in the session — scope discipline is critical; politics and NIMBY dynamics affect every housing initiative; location tracking of residents raises serious privacy concerns; has it been done before needs a thorough answer (Apricot, Clarity Human Services)

**Synergy**
- Playfield (housing crisis as flagship social systems simulation)
- Commonground ("help others experience homelessness" empathy simulation)
- Groundtruth (housing data as civic dataset)
- DataMutants (outcome tracking and impact measurement)
- Walkframe (structured walking as resident wellness program)
