## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — Subtext

**One Phrase** — *Every Document, Revealed*

**Core Sentence** — A multi-document semantic structure extraction and visualization tool that ingests any document corpus — contracts, legal filings, research papers, due diligence packages, property documents — extracts the underlying semantic graph, and renders it as mind map, network graph, plain English audit, JSON profile, or any other visualization depending on what the question requires — making the structure, connections, and gaps inside any document set visible without reading every word.

**One Paragraph** — Every contract and document corpus has a structure — obligations, connections, gaps — that's completely invisible to linear reading. Subtext is an X-ray: it ingests any document set, extracts the underlying semantic graph, and renders it as a plain-English audit, a mind map, a JSON profile, or a cross-reference map depending on the question. For an individual it answers 'what am I actually signing?'; for a deal team it shows how a clause in one document reshapes obligations in another — all without ever practicing law. It shares its ingest engine with Siteframe, so the hard part gets built once and pays off twice.

**Key Insights**
- Every document corpus has a skeleton — Subtext shows it; the structure, connections, obligations, and gaps are already in the documents; they're just invisible to linear reading
- Data/presentation separation at the ecosystem level — the document ingest and semantic extraction engine is shared infrastructure; JSON profile, mind map, network graph, and plain English audit are all render modes of the same revealed structure
- Contract audit as consumer entry point — stripping legalese and confirming desired terms are in the final document; comprehension and confirmation not legal advice
- Cross-document connection mapping is the enterprise value — how a clause in one document affects obligations in another; connections invisible in linear reading become navigable in a semantic graph
- Siteframe ingest layer overlap reveals shared infrastructure — the ingest and structure extraction engine belongs to neither product specifically; it is ecosystem infrastructure
- The X-ray metaphor is precisely right — an X-ray doesn't add anything to what's already there; it reveals internal structure invisible from the outside
- Not practicing law — the guardrail is identical to Estatecoach; comprehension, confirmation, and structure revelation are not legal advice

**User**
- Primary: individuals signing contracts they don't fully understand — leases, employment agreements, service contracts, NDAs
- Secondary: legal professionals managing large document corpora — due diligence, discovery, regulatory compliance
- Tertiary: researchers, analysts, and knowledge workers dealing with large document sets

**Model**
- Consumer freemium — free single document audit; premium for multi-document corpus, cross-reference mapping
- Legal professional tier — higher price point, larger corpus handling
- Enterprise — M&A due diligence, regulatory compliance, contract lifecycle management
- API/engine layer — document ingest and structure extraction engine licensed to Siteframe, ThinkMutant, and other ecosystem products
- DataMutants delivery — Subtext capability deployed for client document intelligence projects

**Why It's Real**
- Contract signing stress is universal
- Legal document complexity is growing not shrinking
- Multi-document cross-reference is expensive human time — automation is technically achievable and commercially valuable
- Siteframe already implies this engine — building it once for Subtext makes Siteframe's document intelligence free
- ThinkMutant provides the visualization layer — Subtext doesn't need to build its own render capability
- Estatecoach precedent — the not-legal-advice guardrail is already established in the ecosystem

**Flags**
- Not practicing law is the critical and non-negotiable guardrail — any drift toward interpretation rather than revelation creates legal and ethical exposure
- Document parsing quality is the core technical challenge — contracts are inconsistently formatted, full of defined terms, cross-references, and exceptions
- Competitive landscape more populated in enterprise — Kira Systems, Luminance, ContractPodAi, Ironclad all operate in legal AI document review
- Consumer market monetization is harder than it looks — individuals who need contract review most are often in low-willingness-to-pay moments (signing a lease under time pressure is not when you reach for your credit card); institutional or employer-sponsored models may be more viable entry points
- Legal professional tier pricing and workflow integration needs validation — legal professionals have existing tools and high switching costs; the API/engine layer may be more natural than a direct product sale
- Shared engine dependency — Subtext depends on the document ingest engine being built and maintained as ecosystem infrastructure; ownership and build responsibility needs explicit decision
- Scope discipline — contracts and legal documents first; research corpora second

**Synergy**
- ThinkMutant — shares visualization engine; Subtext extracts the semantic graph, ThinkMutant renders it
- Siteframe — shared document ingest and structure extraction infrastructure; property documents are one domain expression
- Estatecoach — natural integration; estate planning documents rendered as plain English audit
- InsightMutant — question-asking engine applied to document corpora
- DataMutants — commercial delivery vehicle; document intelligence engagements as DataMutants projects
- Groundtruth — policy and regulatory documents as Subtext input
- The Current — document corpus as a The Current input stream
- ForecastMutant — contract term analysis feeding operational forecasts; lease expiration dates extracted by Subtext feeding ForecastMutant real estate cluster
