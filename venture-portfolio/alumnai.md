## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — Alumn.ai

**One Phrase** — *Your Network, Finally Activated* *(uncertain)*

**Core Sentence** — Transform every school's existing yearbook and alumni data into a living, searchable, privacy-first community that activates the most underutilized social asset most people have — their alumni network — without putting the burden of maintenance on the alumni themselves

**One Paragraph** — Every school is sitting on its most underused social asset — alumni — trapped in yearbooks and stale databases nobody has figured out how to activate. Alumn.ai deconstructs that existing data into a living, searchable, privacy-first community, and the 'photo of back-then plus now' mechanic dissolves the most awkward moment in social life: the who-are-you-again reunion. Tribal affinity — we graduated together — is a real social contract that makes people help across loose ties, and the school pays because reunion attendance and giving have measurable dollar value. The data already exists; nobody has made it useful.

**Key Insights**
- Yearbook deconstruction is the unlock — every school already has the asset; photos, names, activities trapped in a physical object; activation not data collection is the problem
- "Pic of back then + now" mechanic eliminates the most embarrassing moment in human social life — the "who are you again" reunion conversation
- Tribal affinity is the emotional engine — we graduated together creates an inherent willingness to help even across loose ties; that's not nostalgia, that's a social contract
- Granovetter's weak ties theory applies directly — alumni networks are the largest untapped weak tie database most people have
- School incentive alignment is the business model — reunion attendance, annual giving, scholarship endowments all have measurable dollar value; the school pays, alumni benefit
- Yearbook photo rights — copyright clearance is an open unresolved item

**User** — Alumni who want to reconnect meaningfully; schools wanting measurable increases in reunion attendance and annual giving; reunion organizers; development offices, alumni relations departments

**Model** — B2B SaaS (school pays based on alumni database size); reunion activation packages; development office integration; white label school-branded experience; data never sold

**Why It's Real**
- Every school has the underlying asset and has profoundly faceplanted on activation
- Tribal affinity mechanic is culturally universal
- School ROI is documentable and immediate
- LinkedIn and Facebook have proven alumni want to connect — they've built the wrong experience for this use case

**Flags** — Numerous attempts have failed — understanding exactly why is essential before building; yearbook digitization requires rights clearance (copyright clearance unresolved); facial recognition raises privacy concerns; network effects are brutal; alumni data ownership questions need clear policies

**Synergy**
- Phase Next (alumni network activation as reinvention catalyst)
- Walkframe (alumni walking clubs as community activation events)
- Pebble (alumni micro-philanthropy for school projects)
- CobaltBat (alumni network as talent pipeline)
- Selfont (reunion name tags and welcome packets in alumni's own handwriting font; formerly Inkself)
