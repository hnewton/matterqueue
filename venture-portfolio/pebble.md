## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — Pebble

**One Phrase** — *Small Gifts, Real Change* *(uncertain)*

**Core Sentence** — A micro-philanthropy platform that gives small community projects the operational infrastructure, donor stewardship, and experimental rigor that big philanthropy takes for granted — making giving at human scale as trustworthy and measurable as any institutional grant

**One Paragraph** — GoFundMe takes the money and walks away; Pebble takes responsibility for what happens next. It gives small community projects the operational infrastructure, donor stewardship, and experimental rigor that big philanthropy takes for granted — vetting, escrow, retrospectives, and values-based donor-project matching. Tactical urbanism outputs — murals, gardens, playgrounds — are visible, photographable proof that the money mattered. Giving at human scale, made as trustworthy and measurable as any institutional grant.

**Key Insights**
- Donor relationship failure and project execution failure are the same problem — no operational infrastructure for either; one platform solves both
- GoFundMe is a payment processor with a social layer — no vetting, no framework, no measurement, no retrospective, no stewardship
- Experimental mentality is the design principle — try this in 5 spots, run a real retrospective, learn and iterate
- Vector database donor-project matching by values alignment not category browsing
- Tactical urbanism outputs — murals, gardens, playgrounds — are visible, photographable proof points
- You have lived both sides — failed donor relationships as a trustee, micro-project potential as a community observer

**User** — Individual donors wanting confidence their money matters; community project initiators; cities wanting to fast-track micro-projects; community foundations, local businesses

**Model** — Platform fee on successful funding (small, trust-preserving); city partnership model; matching grant marketplace; earn to give (volunteer hours to giving credits); subscription stewardship tier

**Why It's Real**
- Nonprofit donor stewardship failure is universal and documentable — you've lived it as a trustee
- Micro-project execution failure is equally universal — passion without infrastructure produces abandoned projects
- GoFundMe, Patreon, and Watsi each solve one piece; nobody has assembled the full stack
- Tactical urbanism movement has practitioners, projects, and energy — they need infrastructure not inspiration

**Flags** — Trust system is the entire product — one fraud destroys credibility; vetting and escrow mechanics need to be airtight from day one; geographic focus question is real; differentiation from Watsi, Neighborly, and ioby needs sharp articulation

**Synergy**
- Uncard (thank you card mechanic built into platform; every funded project generates handwritten-font thank you via Selfont)
- Selfont (donor stewardship closes loop with a product you already own; formerly Inkself)
- Groundtruth (community impact data as civic dataset)
- Walkframe (walking club as Pebble-fundable micro-project)
