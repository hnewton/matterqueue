## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — Syllably

**One Phrase** — *Your Syllabus, Actually Useful* *(uncertain)*

**Core Sentence** — Eliminate the millions of hours students waste manually transferring syllabus information into their own systems by making any syllabus machine-readable, beautifully structured, and instantly integrated with the tools students already use

**One Paragraph** — Every student at every university retypes the same syllabus data into their own systems every semester — a global, universal, entirely unnecessary waste of millions of hours. Syllably makes any syllabus machine-readable and instantly integrated with the tools students already use, via a Chrome extension that needs zero institutional buy-in. The accessibility and UDL angle is a quiet backdoor to campus adoption, and the highest-need users are exactly the students EF Coach serves. The prototype already extracts Canvas assignments and pushes them straight to a to-do system — it's now a foregrounded capability inside EF Coach.

**Key Insights**
- Every student at every university manually retypes the same syllabus data every semester — that's a global, universal, and entirely unnecessary pain
- Chrome extension is the right entry point — no institutional buy-in, no professor behavior change, student installs and it works on any syllabus anywhere
- UDL and accessibility angle is a compliance requirement at most universities — backdoor to institutional adoption
- Executive Function connection — highest need users are students with ADHD and learning differences
- Professor-side smart syllabus builder that outputs beautiful PDF and structured data simultaneously solves both sides at once
- PLG motion is clean — student is both user and distribution channel

**User** — College students; students with ADHD and learning differences; professors wanting better syllabus design; disability services offices, academic success centers

**Model** — Free Chrome extension (PLG); freemium for advanced integrations; institutional licensing for universities; CobaltBat PLG funnel for early professional acquisition

**Why It's Real**
- Pain is universal, repeated every semester, entirely unambiguous
- Chrome extension requires zero institutional approval
- UDL compliance creates institutional pull without cold outreach
- Auto planning output (syllabus in, semester calendar out) is immediately and obviously valuable

**Flags** — Syllabus formats are wildly inconsistent — parsing reliability is the core technical challenge; only changes a few times per semester — value per use needs to be very high; institutional sales cycles are long

**Synergy**
- EF Coach (students with learning differences)
- CobaltBat (student to professional pipeline; path unconfirmed)
- EarlyRead (students identified through EarlyRead become Syllably users)
- Phase Next (adult learners returning to education)
