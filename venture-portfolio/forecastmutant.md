## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — ForecastMutant

**One Phrase** — *Your Data, Plus Everyone's*

**Core Sentence** — A calibrated probabilistic forecasting platform that combines an organization's own historical data with cooperative fingerprint matching across a contributor network — producing living forecasts across temporal, spatial, and semantic domains from demand and inventory to staffing, agriculture, law, sports, and civic — with the Waze cooperative model as the structural innovation and Palantir-for-SMB as the positioning.

**One Paragraph** — Palantir gives big organizations the power to make their operational data legible and act on it; everyone else gets spreadsheets and gut feel. ForecastMutant closes that gap — a calibrated forecasting platform that blends an organization's own history with cooperative fingerprint matching across a contributor network, the Waze model applied to operations. The buyer sees three scenario cards and a recommendation; the sixteen-layer engine stays invisible underneath, and a public calibration track record is the trust product. The network is the moat, because algorithms commoditize and a co-op where everyone gets back more than they put in does not.

**Key Insights**
- Palantir for SMB is the sharpest positioning — same operational complexity, same uncertainty, none of the infrastructure; the gap is real and large
- The cooperative network is the value, not the algorithm — algorithms commoditize; a contributor network where each member gets back more signal than they put in does not; this is the Waze model applied to operational forecasting
- Three prediction mechanisms always run together — extrapolation (your own history), fingerprint matching (orgs whose patterns resemble yours via SignalMutant/PatternMutant), simulation/scenario (bear/base/bull counterfactuals)
- Three forecast types expand domain coverage — temporal (time-series), spatial (where will demand or patterns concentrate geographically), semantic (which categories or concepts will gain or lose relevance); all three run on the same engine
- Cognitive bias is a product layer — anchoring, recency bias, overconfidence, and narrative fallacy systematically distort forecasts; InsightMutant's questioning engine applied to forecast assumptions is the implementation
- Calibration is the accountability mechanism — a forecast is accurate if stated confidence matches actual hit rate over time; the calibration track record is the trust product
- Simple surface, complex engine — the SMB buyer needs three scenario cards and a recommendation; the 16-layer architecture is invisible underneath
- Uncertainty communication is a product decision — scenario cards (bear/base/bull) with calibration track record as primary trust product; the failure modes of weather forecast communication (false precision, false confidence, paralysis, ignored uncertainty) are explicitly avoided; stating confidence that matches actual hit rate over time is the accountability mechanism

**User**
- Primary: SMB and midmarket organizations with operational forecasting needs and underutilized historical data — demand planners, operations managers, agricultural operators, clinic administrators
- Secondary: vertical specialists — sports analytics teams, legal operations, civic and government agencies
- Tertiary: platform buyers wanting to embed forecast capability — R3 Mutation Engine licensing

**Model**
- R1 Living Forecast — subscription for continuously updating forecast
- R4 Network Effect — cooperative contribution unlocks richer signal; contributors pay less or get more based on contribution quality
- R6 Knowledge Marketplace — consultant domain packs; vertical specialists sell domain expertise through the platform
- R3 Mutation Engine — PatternMutant and SignalMutant licensed as infrastructure to other products
- Prediction market surface — distinct product layer; later phase; underserved domain selection (regional demand, enrollment, local outcomes) is the differentiator
- Video summary delivery — premium output format; 60-second forecast explainer with scenario cards, key assumptions, and what would change the picture; shareable and differentiating; no current SMB forecasting tool does this
- R7 Cold Start Engine — prerequisite, not a configuration; cooperative model requires critical mass per domain; first catalog domain must be narrow, specific, findable (5-10 contributing orgs), easy data; cold start is the existential challenge and must be solved before any other feature

**Why It's Real**
- Deep existing sessions with 16-layer architecture, 66 use cases documented across 9 clusters
- Waze cooperative model has proven precedent
- Palantir-for-SMB gap is real and documentable
- PatternMutant shared with HypothesisEngine — build once, two products immediately
- Calibration track record as trust product is defensible and differentiating

**Flags**

- SignalMutant naming collision with CobaltBat — needs resolution before architecture locks
- Vertical vs. horizontal go-to-market not resolved

**Synergy**

- DiffMutant — drift detection across forecast streams; when forecast consistently diverges from actuals, DiffMutant surfaces the regime change
- InsightMutant — cognitive bias correction layer; questioning engine applied to forecast assumptions
- HeadSim — shared cognitive bias taxonomy; behavioral traps in investing and in forecasting are the same traps named differently; HeadSim was formerly Mindmarket
- DataMutants — commercial delivery vehicle
- CobaltBat — SignalMutant engine overlap; architecture decision needed
- MapMutant — spatial forecasting rendering layer; ForecastMutant produces the prediction, MapMutant visualizes where and how it's changing
- Groundtruth — civic and government forecast domains
- Siteframe — real estate cluster; lease expiration and tenant demand forecasting
- EarlyRead — health and education clusters; learning indicator pattern detection over time
