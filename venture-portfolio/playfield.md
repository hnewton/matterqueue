## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — Playfield

**One Phrase** — *Turn Your Mission Into a Game* *(uncertain)*

**Core Sentence** — Give nonprofits and social ventures a no-code open source engine to turn their domain knowledge into games and simulations that educate, build empathy, and fundraise — built by a nonprofit for nonprofits

**One Paragraph** — Nonprofits have deep domain knowledge and no way to make it interactive — Playfield is the no-code engine that turns their mission into games and simulations that educate, build empathy, and fundraise. The mechanic is systemic simulation: players inhabit multiple stakeholder roles and feel why a system is hard, the way Papers Please and Spent create complicity instead of lectures. The engine is the product and the games are infinite output — one tool, endless domains from housing to climate to immigration. Built by a nonprofit, for nonprofits, so the vehicle and the message are the same thing.

**Key Insights**
- Nonprofits have domain knowledge; they lack the ability to make it interactive — clean division of labor
- Systemic simulation is the mechanic — players inhabit multiple stakeholder roles and feel why the system is hard
- The engine is the product; the games are the output — infinite content without infinite development
- Karmaware/donateware as optional consumer model — any app built on Playfield can route proceeds to its cause
- The org that builds it is a nonprofit/social venture itself — vehicle and message are the same thing
- Open source core with sustainability funding is the honest model — Mozilla/Wikipedia/Signal are structural precedents

**User** — Nonprofits, social ventures, civic education organizations, policy think tanks, community organizers, universities

**Model** — Open source core; sustainability via grants, donations, light licensing for premium features; flagship game as reference and sales tool

**Why It's Real**
- Nonprofits have budget, grants, and genuine need
- No-code scenario builder is buildable — define stakeholders, constraints, tradeoffs; engine handles rest
- Best systemic games (Papers Please, Spent) are remembered because they create complicity not lectures
- Housing, climate, healthcare, immigration, criminal justice — one engine, infinite domains

**Flags** — Nonprofit sales cycles are slow; game design quality still matters even with good content; open source sustainability requires disciplined governance; needs anchor nonprofit partner before building

**Synergy**
- Groundtruth (shared factual infrastructure)
- Commonground (empathy building through systemic simulation)
- Shelterframe (housing crisis as flagship Playfield game)
- DataMutants (civic data delivery partner)
