## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — Uncard

**One Phrase** — *Cards for the Unspeakable* *(uncertain)*

**Core Sentence** — An anti-greeting card line for occasions, emotions, and relationships the card industry is too cowardly to acknowledge — starting with the dark humor of dementia caregiving and expanding to every unspeakable moment that deserves a card

**One Paragraph** — Stand at a Hallmark rack during something genuinely hard — a parent's dementia, say — and none of the cards are right, because the industry is too cowardly to acknowledge the moment. Uncard is the anti-greeting-card line for exactly those occasions, starting with the dark, true humor of caregiving, printed on demand with a dollar from every card going to research. The origin — real things a mother actually said — is impossible to manufacture or compete with, and every card is its own Instagram-native marketing asset. The card you couldn't find at Hallmark, finally.

**Key Insights**
- Ghost kitchen model for cards — no retail, no spinner rack, no Hallmark deal; sharp creative, print on demand, direct delivery
- "Things my Mom said" origin story is the soul of the product — specific, real, funny in the way only painful things can be; impossible to manufacture or compete with
- Caregiver community is the core audience — isolated, exhausted, desperately needing permission to laugh at impossible situations
- $1 per card to dementia research is a clean built-in giving mechanic
- The card IS the gift — right card for an unspeakable occasion needs no upsell

**User** — Dementia caregivers; anyone who has stood at a Hallmark rack thinking none of these are right; dark humor enthusiasts

**Model** — Print on demand (zero inventory); $1 per card to cause; community submissions with microfinance royalty; buy one fund one; membership; AI-generated illustration

**Why It's Real**
- Print on demand infrastructure is mature
- White space is genuine — pharmacy card racks are a creative wasteland
- Caregiver community is large, underserved, and word-of-mouth driven
- Origin story is authentic and specific — impossible to manufacture
- Instagram-native design means every card is its own marketing asset

**Flags** — Humor has to be sharp enough to land without being cruel; editorial taste is the entire product; Rachel's list is the first test; dementia humor walks a line

**Synergy**
- Selfont (handwriting font integration; formerly Inkself)
- Pebble ($1 giving mechanic model)
- Phase Next (caregiver community in 50-something demographic)
- Patterntopia (card design aesthetics as generative ambient art — following Flowscape absorption)
