## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — HeadSim

**One Phrase** — *Simulate the Mind, Not the Market* *(uncertain)*

**Core Sentence** — A cognitive simulation platform that trains the psychological and behavioral meta-skills of high-stakes decision-making through scenario-based decisions that name and defuse cognitive traps in real time — with investing as the initial content domain and a coaching layer powered by InsightMutant that extends the model to any domain where expert judgment under uncertainty is the product

**One Paragraph** — Every costly decision mistake has a name — loss aversion, anchoring, sunk cost — but you don't unlearn them from a lecture, you unlearn them from reps. HeadSim is a cognitive flight simulator: you make high-stakes decisions under realistic pressure in a safe place where failure is instructive, and an InsightMutant-powered coach debriefs not whether you were right but what you were thinking. Investing is the first content domain and the most personally motivated, but the same engine trains medical, legal, and crisis judgment. Practice the mind, not the market.

**Key Insights**
- Simulator layer is the reframe — the first prototype felt like a card game; the right model is a cognitive flight simulator; you practice decision-making under realistic pressure in a safe environment where failure is instructive not costly
- Investing was the origin but is not the ceiling — the simulator mechanic applies to any domain where expert judgment under uncertainty is the skill: investing, medical diagnosis, legal strategy, military command, crisis management, sports coaching, negotiation, clinical psychology; each domain has the same structure: incomplete information, time pressure, cognitive bias, consequential decision
- InsightMutant as coaching layer — the questioning engine applied post-decision: not "you were wrong" but "what were you thinking when you made that call?"; surfaces the reasoning process, not just the outcome; this is the Gary Klein recognition-primed decision model applied as an AI coaching experience
- Gary Klein expertise model to review — Klein's research on how experts actually make decisions (recognition-primed decision model, naturalistic decision making) is directly applicable to HeadSim's coaching layer design; this is an open research item before the coaching layer is built
- Incomplete information is the mechanic — each scenario forces a decision the way real situations do, with debrief explaining the cognitive trap triggered; the debrief is where learning lives
- Named biases become recognizable through reps not lectures — loss aversion, recency bias, anchoring, narrative fallacy, sunk cost, overconfidence; repetition across scenarios builds pattern recognition
- 15-20 minute constraint keeps it as a skill trainer not a full simulation — respects the learner's time; deep enough to be meaningful, short enough to be habitual
- Domain expansion model — investing scenarios are the proof of concept; medical diagnosis scenarios, legal strategy scenarios, and crisis management scenarios all follow the same template; domain packs are the expansion model
- Coaching experience as UX — HeadSim is not just a game with a debrief; it is a coaching relationship that happens to use simulation as its primary method; the AI coach asks the right questions at the right moment; this is InsightMutant's question-asking pattern applied to skill development

**User**
- Primary: active investors, finance students, investment clubs — the origin community; already motivated, already tracking their own biases
- Secondary: professionals in high-stakes decision domains — medical residents, legal trainees, crisis managers, military officers, sports coaches; training contexts with existing budget
- Tertiary: general adult learners interested in decision quality and cognitive bias recognition across life domains

**Model**
- Core scenario engine: free or freemium — investing domain as acquisition surface
- Domain packs: premium content for investing (bear markets, earnings seasons, crypto volatility), medical, legal, crisis management, sports coaching
- Institutional licensing: medical schools, law schools, military training programs, corporate leadership development
- Coaching subscription: InsightMutant-powered post-session debrief and longitudinal bias profile
- Physical deck: investing domain as standalone product; low production barrier; proof of concept

**Why It's Real**
- Prototype ran — play engine was challenging but follow-on discussion produced the reframe; the concept is substantially stronger after the prototype than before
- Behavioral finance is well-researched but almost entirely absent from interactive training — the gap is real and documentable
- Gary Klein's naturalistic decision making research provides academic foundation for the coaching layer design
- Investing is the most personally motivated starting point — you are the target user and first tester
- Domain expansion model means one engine serves multiple markets without rebuilding
- HypothesisEngine connection is direct — behavioral biases are both the trading edge source and the HeadSim cognitive trap curriculum; same library, two products

**Flags**
- Play engine was challenging in prototype — initial attempt felt overly complicated; the reframe to simulator rather than card game may resolve this but needs validation
- Gary Klein review is an open prerequisite — the coaching layer design depends on understanding Klein's recognition-primed decision model before building; do not build the coaching layer without this research
- Scenario design is harder than it looks — scenarios need to feel like genuine dilemmas not obvious traps; fine line between educational and preachy; needs playtesting early and often
- Domain expertise for non-investing scenarios — medical, legal, and military scenarios require subject matter validation; cannot be designed from the outside without SME review
- InsightMutant architecture is not yet defined — the coaching layer depends on InsightMutant's concept being developed into buildable architecture first

**Synergy**
- InsightMutant — primary coaching engine; post-decision questioning is InsightMutant's pattern applied to skill development; HeadSim is a high-priority InsightMutant deployment surface
- HypothesisEngine — behavioral bias is both the trading edge source (stop hunts, bull/bear traps) and the HeadSim cognitive trap curriculum; same bias library, two products
- ForecastMutant — shared cognitive bias taxonomy; bias correction in forecasting and in investment decisions are the same problem named differently
- Flaglore, Whenivia, Pawpath — cognitive cluster; shared user base
- EF Coach — executive function and decision quality overlap significantly; shared user
- Phase Next — cognitive maintenance and skill development for 50-somethings; HeadSim as a Phase Next program component
