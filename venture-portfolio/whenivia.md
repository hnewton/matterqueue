## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — Whenivia

**One Phrase** — *When Did That Happen?* *(uncertain)*

**Core Sentence** — Train and test chronological memory through rapid fire date-guessing across multiple content domains — movies, sports, music, news, politics, cars, watches, and more — with proximity scoring that rewards closeness not just accuracy, proven multiplayer mode, and a mobile-first target

**One Paragraph** — Whenivia isn't about right or wrong — it's about how close. It's rapid-fire date guessing across movies, music, sports, cars, watches and more, with proximity scoring that makes being a year off feel different from a decade off, plus a proven multiplayer mode. Each content domain is also an affinity community and a distribution channel — a watches deck reaches the same obsessives ShapeSake serves. The core loop is validated, the expansion to new domains is nearly free, and chronological memory happens to be a real cognitive skill worth keeping sharp.

**Key Insights**
- "How close" proximity scoring is more interesting than right/wrong — one year off feels different than a decade off; the mechanic was validated in prototype
- Chronological memory is a distinct cognitive skill — temporal ordering, not just recall — and it degrades early in cognitive decline
- Multiplayer mode proven in prototype — two-player competitive mode works; social mechanic compounds engagement
- Multi-domain expansion is the ceiling — movies were the prototype entry point; sports, music, news, politics, cars, watches, guitars, sneakers, planes, tech all carry the same "when did that happen?" mechanic naturally
- Content domain = affinity group = distribution channel — a watches domain serves the same passionate community that ShapeSake serves; a guitars domain serves guitar collectors; domains are not just content, they are user acquisition surfaces
- Streak-based rapid fire mechanic rewards consistency and creates flow state
- Timeline mode tests relative memory vs absolute — meaningfully different skill
- Decade heat map reveals personal knowledge blind spots
- PLG surface for The Current — chronological memory content is a natural capture stream; connection is opportunistic not architectural
- Mobile app target — prototype validated the core loop; mobile is the right form factor for rapid fire daily habit

**User** — Trivia lovers, movie obsessives, pop culture nerds; sports fans, watch enthusiasts, car collectors, music obsessives — each domain unlocks a distinct affinity community; people interested in gentle cognitive maintenance; educators, clinicians

**Model** — Free app with multi-domain content; domain packs as premium content; affinity community licensing (branded domain packs for watch forums, car clubs, guitar communities); potential cognitive health institutional angle later

**Why It's Real**
- Prototype worked well — core mechanic validated; multiplayer proven; mobile target confirmed
- Multi-domain expansion is low incremental cost — same engine, new data sources
- Each domain has passionate users who want to test their knowledge and share results
- Proximity scoring creates significantly more satisfying experience than binary right/wrong
- Streak and heat map mechanics create natural return habit loop
- Affinity domain connection to ShapeSake is direct — same communities, different product moment

**Flags** — Dozens of trivia apps exist; differentiation lives in proximity scoring, timeline mechanic, and multi-domain affinity targeting; cognitive health claims need to stay soft unless clinically validated; domain data quality varies — movie and sports data excellent, niche collector domains require curation

**Synergy**
- Flaglore, HeadSim, Pawpath (cognitive cluster, shared user)
- ShapeSake — direct affinity community overlap; watches, guitars, cars, sneakers, planes domains serve identical passion communities; cross-promotion natural
- The Current — chronological content as a capture stream; PLG connection opportunistic not architectural
- Phase Next — temporal memory and cognitive maintenance angle; 50-something demographic overlap
