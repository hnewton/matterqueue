## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — EF Coach

**One Phrase** — [FILL: one phrase — the sharpest distillation]

**Core Sentence** — An AI coach that helps people with executive function challenges — ADHD, learning differences, overwhelm — use AI differently: not just as a tool but as a thinking partner for daily reviews, plans, and tracking that adapt to how their mind actually works

**One Paragraph** — Most productivity apps are built for people who don't need them — they assume the executive function that ADHD, learning differences, and overwhelm make unreliable. EF Coach is an AI coach that works the way these minds actually work: a thinking partner for the daily reviews, forward plans, and progress tracking where executive function breaks down most, not another to-do list to abandon. Powered by InsightMutant's questioning engine, it scaffolds initiation and reflection instead of just storing tasks. It's the highest personal-impact idea in the portfolio, with the clearest user.

**Key Insights**
- Executive function is the meta-skill — planning, initiation, working memory, emotional regulation, flexibility; coaching these through AI is fundamentally different from productivity apps that assume neurotypical execution
- AI as thinking partner not task manager — the distinction matters enormously for this user; most productivity tools are built for people who don't need them
- Daily reviews and plans are the core product loops — weekly review, forward planning, and progress tracking are where EF breaks down most consistently and where AI can scaffold most effectively; these are foregrounded, not secondary
- Decision making and critical thinking — named mode and content layer within EF Coach; not a separate capsule
- InsightMutant is the questioning engine — EF Coach is the primary deployment surface
