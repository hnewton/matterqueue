## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — Commonground

**One Phrase** — *Feel First, Then Facts* *(uncertain)*

**Core Sentence** — Help people genuinely understand opposing viewpoints by leading with human story and shared values before introducing facts — building empathy and critical thinking in the sequence that actually works

**One Paragraph** — Showing people they're wrong doesn't change their minds — it hardens them, because the problem was never an information deficit. Commonground flips the sequence the research says actually works: human story and shared values first, facts second, so you feel something about a person before you'll update a belief because of them. Steelmanning — arguing the other side as strongly as you can — becomes a teachable, scorable mechanic, and most factual disagreement turns out narrower than the perceived kind. The hardest problem in the set, and also the most fundable and most needed.

**Key Insights**
- Information deficit is not the problem — motivation to engage charitably is; showing contradicting facts rarely changes minds and often hardens them
- Sequencing is everything — human story and relationship before facts; feel something about a person before you'll update a belief because of them
- Backfire effect, motivated reasoning, and identity-protective cognition are well-documented mechanisms working against every naive fact-first depolarization tool
- Most effective depolarization work (Braver Angels, Living Room Conversations, StoryCorps One Small Step) leads with story not data
- Steelmanning as a mechanic — argue the other side's position as strongly as possible before critiquing it
- Factual disagreement is almost always narrower than perceived disagreement

**User** — Intellectually honest people who suspect their own bubble is limiting them; educators teaching critical thinking; facilitators running structured dialogue

**Model** — Nonprofit or open source; grant funding (democracy, civic tech foundations); institutional licensing; Braver Angels and Living Room Conversations as coalition partners

**Why It's Real**
- Depolarization is one of the most actively funded civic tech categories right now
- The sequencing insight — story before facts — is research-backed and almost universally ignored by existing tools
- Steelmanning mechanic is teachable, gamifiable, and measurable
- Groundtruth provides the shared factual infrastructure

**Flags** — Depolarization tool graveyard is real; self-selection problem is severe; measuring attitude change is hard and expensive; avoiding false balance is as hard here as in Groundtruth

**Synergy**
- Groundtruth (direct and essential; shared factual infrastructure underneath empathy layer)
- Playfield (systemic simulation as experiential empathy building)
- HeadSim (behavioral bias recognition maps to motivated reasoning; formerly Mindmarket)
