## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — CobaltBat

**One Phrase** — Reboot Recruiting

**Core Sentence** — An AI-powered talent matching platform with ResumeMutant as the PLG entry point — giving candidates a genuinely useful resume and professional identity tool while building the semantic matching infrastructure that makes employer-candidate fit meaningful rather than keyword-dependent; two-sided marketplace deferred until both sides have traction

**One Paragraph** — Hiring still runs on keyword matching, which is why great candidates and the right employers keep missing each other. CobaltBat starts where the value is obvious — ResumeMutant, a genuinely useful resume and professional-identity tool candidates want on day one — and quietly builds the semantic matching layer underneath that makes employer-candidate fit mean something. The same matching engine powers Siteframe and Alumn.ai, so it gets built once and earns three times. Start with the tool people love and let the marketplace emerge from traction rather than forcing both sides at once.

**Key Insights**
- ResumeMutant is the PLG entry point — candidates get a tool they want immediately; no marketplace required to start; employer side follows when candidate traction exists
- Semantic matching engine is the core asset — same engine that powers Siteframe's tenant-property matching and Alumn.ai's alumni connection; build once across all three
- Two-sided marketplace deferred — building both sides simultaneously before either has traction is the classic marketplace trap; ResumeMutant first, TalentMutant when employer demand is evidenced
- College student acquisition angle remains unconfirmed — Syllably to CobaltBat pipeline is a hypothesis not a designed mechanism; alternative acquisition paths needed in parallel
