## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — Framelab

**One Phrase** — *The Photography Suite That Actually Teaches* *(uncertain — may expand as platform grows)*

**Core Sentence** — A suite of micro-apps that compress the learning curve for photographers and visual creatives through real-time simulation, AI-guided planning, and personality-driven feedback — photography-first, with a clear expansion path toward a visual education platform across data science, investing, design, video, and other visually-driven domains

**One Paragraph** — See the shot before you take it, and understand why after. Framelab compresses the photography learning curve with a suite of micro-apps — an exposure-triangle simulator that runs on your own photo, AI-guided pre-shoot planning, and personality-driven feedback, imagine Annie Leibovitz reviewing your lighting. The exposure-on-your-own-image trick is the emotional unlock, and the same template generalizes to any domain with visual structure: data science, design, investing, video. Prove it with photographers first; the visual-education platform is the ceiling.

**Key Insights**
- Exposure triangle simulator on YOUR photo is the emotional unlock — your own image creates investment that makes learning stick; this is the template for every FrameLab simulation: take something from the learner's real context, let them manipulate it, explain what's happening
- Famous editor personality layer for shoot review is the most original combination — Annie Leibovitz on your lighting choices; only possible because Persona exists; personality-driven feedback is what separates FrameLab from every other photography education tool
- Question-asking engine applied to pre-shoot planning surfaces what photographers systematically under-plan; this is the coaching/question experience engine applied to creative planning
- StyleVault / moodboard as native pre-production surface — visual reference gathering before the shoot is a natural FrameLab planning layer; the same pre-production moodboard pattern applies to video projects, architectural walkthroughs, garden planning, and other field-based visual activities; StyleVault / The Current style stream is the natural home for this
- Suite instinct is right — student uses simulator, working photographer uses planner, photojournalist uses editor feedback; each tool valuable standalone, dramatically more valuable together
- Visual education platform is the expansion ceiling — photography is the proof-of-concept vertical; the exposure triangle simulator template generalizes to any domain with visual structure and manipulable variables:
- **Data science**: network analysis visualization, viral/epidemic models (SIR, SIS, SEIR) where parameter manipulation makes the curve behavior intuitive; decision boundaries, distributions, overfitting — all visual and manipulable
- **Investing and behavioral finance**: HeadSim connection direct; cognitive bias scenarios with debrief; pattern recognition training on historical market structures
- **Design education**: typography, color theory, composition; manipulate variables, see results in real time
- **Video production**: frame rate, aspect ratio, color grading, compression artifacts — same simulation model applied to motion
- **Architecture and spatial design**: light simulation, proportion, shadow modeling, material behavior
- **Biology and anatomy**: cell processes, organ systems, evolutionary relationships — visual with manipulable parameters
- **Physics**: pendulum, wave interference, orbital mechanics
- **Music theory**: waveform, harmony, chord structure rendered as interactive visualization
- **Urban planning**: traffic flow, zoning impact, density — connects to MapMutant
- Field process management is a distinct expansion direction — photography shoot management (pre/during/post workflow) is a process app; the same pattern applies to video production, site surveys, architectural documentation, field data collection; this drifts toward SaaS/workflow territory but the "visual activity you do in the field" frame is distinctive
- Photo → composition analysis: rule of thirds, leading lines, light direction identified and annotated — a FrameLab-specific application of the photo → structured data pipeline
- Visual education market is substantially larger than photographer headcount alone — every data science student, design student, and investing learner is a potential user if the platform expands

**User**
- Primary: serious amateur photographers; working photographers and photojournalists; photography educators
- Secondary (expansion): data science students and practitioners wanting visual intuition tools; design students; investing learners
- Tertiary: videographers, visual journalists, creative directors; any learner in a domain where seeing beats reading

**Model**
- Freemium suite (photography-first): exposure simulator free; advanced tools and AI feedback premium
- Educator licensing: photography schools, design programs, data science bootcamps
- Persona marketplace integration: editor and domain expert personality modules
- Visual education platform (expansion): domain-specific simulation modules sold as premium packs
- Field process management (expansion): shoot workflow, video production workflow as SaaS subscription
- StyleVault / The Current integration: pre-production moodboard as native planning surface

**Why It's Real**
- Prototype worked well — validates core mechanic; simulation approach confirmed
- Exposure triangle simulator is technically straightforward — the insight is the UX not the engineering
- Photoshoot under-planning is universal and documentable
- Persona infrastructure concept developed — editor personality modules are natural FrameLab feature
- Visual education is chronically underserved by interactive tools — across photography, data science, design, and more
- Data science visualization gap is real — SIR models and network analysis are taught with equations but understood through visualization; no good interactive tools exist for learners

**Flags**
- Photography market is passionate but not massive — expansion toward visual education platform is the right direction but needs photography validation first; do not expand before photography vertical is proven
- Editor personality modules require careful copyright and likeness handling; Persona architecture not yet defined — coaching/question experience engine concept at early stage
- Suite coherence requires disciplined product vision; simulation accuracy needs to be genuinely good
- Field process management direction drifts toward SaaS/workflow — worth naming as a future direction but not the first build
- Connection to The Current is optional not architectural

**Synergy**
- Persona / coaching experience engine — personality-driven feedback layer; editor modules are the most compelling FrameLab feature; Persona may be a configuration layer within the coaching engine rather than a standalone product — sequencing decision open
- InsightMutant / coaching experience — question-asking engine powers pre-shoot planning; same coaching pattern applies to data science project planning, investing scenario setup
- StyleVault / The Current — pre-production moodboard is a native FrameLab planning surface; The Current's style stream is the right home for visual references; not architectural but deeply natural
- HeadSim — investing simulation and pattern recognition are natural FrameLab expansion content; behavioral finance visualization connects both products
- DataMutants — data science visualization as a FrameLab expansion domain; DataMutants engagements could use FrameLab visual education tools
