## Idea Capsule — 2026-04-01

**Name** — Hollowframe
**One Phrase** — A peripheral display layer for ambient awareness
**Core Sentence** — Hollowframe is a dedicated peripheral display that shows environmental and project state at the edge of your visual field — present without demanding focus.
**One Paragraph** — Hollowframe started as a question: what if the information you needed most was always visible but never in the way? A strip of light that shifts color with your calendar density. A number at the corner of your screen that shows your team's current focus. Not notifications — presence. Hollowframe became the prototype for what eventually became Glassbell's experience layer.

**Key Insights**
- Peripheral display works — the prototype confirmed people notice ambient change without feeling interrupted
- Trying to build both capture and display in one product was too broad — the display layer is where the value is
- The hardware vs software tradeoff is real: a browser tab works but a dedicated display is better

**User** — Knowledge workers who find notification systems cognitively expensive.

**Model** — Hardware product + software subscription. (Never fully resolved before absorption.)

**Why It's Real**
- Prototype validation confirmed the core interaction model
- Physical ambient displays (Ambient Orb, Nanoleaf) have proven the concept in hardware

**Flags**
- Hardware dependency made the go-to-market path complex
- Scope was too broad — capture + display is two products

**Synergy**
- **Glassbell** — Hollowframe's peripheral display insight was the seed of Glassbell's experience layer
