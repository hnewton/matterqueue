## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — Siteframe

**One Phrase** — [FILL: one phrase — the sharpest distillation]

**Core Sentence** — A PLG property intelligence platform that gives brokers and owners a free 3D siteplan tool and suite-level microdb in exchange for building the inventory layer that powers a semantic commercial real estate marketplace — without ripping out CoStar, just making it irrelevant at the edges

**One Paragraph** — Commercial real estate decisions happen at the suite level, but the data that exists is stuck at the building level — CoStar's world. Siteframe gives brokers and owners a free 3D siteplan tool they actually want, and in exchange quietly builds the suite-level inventory layer that powers a semantic marketplace — no rip-and-replace, just making the incumbent irrelevant at the edges. 'Accurate enough' beats forensic precision here, and the owner reporting dashboard creates a flywheel where the data maintains itself. The cold-start problem that kills marketplaces is solved by a tool people use before any marketplace exists.

**Key Insights**
- Free 3D siteplan is the PLG hook — broker gets immediate value, platform gets property data; nobody feels recruited, marketplace emerges from accumulated inventory
- "Accurate enough" is the right accuracy target — spatial intuition for brokers and owners, not forensic precision for engineers; Matterport solves the wrong problem
- Survey/site map import as geometric anchor is the technical shortcut — accuracy from the survey, aesthetics from the photos
- Suite-level microdb is the most differentiated asset — CoStar operates at building level; actual tenant decisions happen at suite level; that granularity almost never exists in structured queryable form
- Owner reporting dashboard creates a flywheel — owners maintain their own suite data as a side effect of using it for asset management
- Semantic matching engine underneath — InsightMutant question experience defines tenant needs at depth; match against properties at same semantic level
- Subtext ingest layer overlap — what Siteframe does with property documents is the same operation as what Subtext does with any document corpus; the ingest and structure extraction engine belongs to neither product specifically

**User** — Commercial real estate brokers; property owners and asset managers; tenants; emerging marketplaces and city planning departments

**Model** — Free 3D siteplan tool (PLG acquisition); suite microdb and reporting dashboard (freemium); marketplace listing fees; semantic matching as premium service; data licensing

**Why It's Real**
- PLG mechanic is clean — value exchange is honest and immediate on both sides
- Suite-level data gap is real and documentable
- Photo-to-3D pipeline is technically mature enough
- Owner reporting is a genuine unmet need — CoStar's incentives are aligned with brokers not owners
- Inventory cold start problem solved by PLG tool

**Flags** — Multi-story and complex structures push photogrammetry limits — scope to single-family, retail, light commercial, low-rise multifamily initially; CoStar has deep broker relationships; suite-level data maintenance requires owner behavior change; CoStar may already have suite-level data in some markets

**Synergy**
- InsightMutant (tenant needs definition at semantic depth)
- The Current (ongoing tenant and owner relationship memory)
- Manhole/Formcap (shared computer vision infrastructure)
- DataMutants (suite-level data cleaning and analysis)
- MapMutant (geographic context layer; Siteframe is property intelligence, MapMutant is geographic context — complements not duplicates)
- Subtext (shared document ingest and structure extraction infrastructure; property documents are one domain expression)
- CBRE — potential external partner
