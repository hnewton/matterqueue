## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — City Lights

**One Phrase** — [FILL: one phrase — the sharpest distillation]

**Core Sentence** — A first-person ambient flying experience through real city lighting data at night — not a navigation tool, not a full 3D city renderer, but a visceral ambient exploration of how cities look and feel from above after dark

**One Paragraph** — City Lights is a visceral, first-person flight through real city lighting data at night — not navigation, not a full 3D renderer, just the feeling of how a city looks and breathes from above after dark. Real-time data makes it alive rather than modeled, and neighborhood granularity is the emotional differentiator: a creative district at 2am and the financial core at the same moment are completely different experiences. Real street and building data add an authenticity purely generative scenes lack. It lives most naturally as ambient content within Patterntopia, a strong Roku and Apple TV candidate.

**Key Insights**
- Real-time API data makes it alive not modeled — occupied buildings, busy streets, active events reflected in light patterns
- Neighborhood granularity is the emotional differentiator — Williamsburg at 2am and the Financial District at the same moment are completely different experiences
- Epic City Sample proves the technical approach for photorealistic urban environments — reference for Patterntopia ambient city contexts
- Ambient and meditative — same emotional register as Patterntopia ambient/drift contexts
- Real neighborhood data is encouraging — streets, buildings, and city structure from real-world data sources is feasible and adds authenticity that purely generative scenes lack
- Unity Sentis or other engines may be better choices than pure Unreal for this use case — prototype revealed canvas limitations; engine decision is open
