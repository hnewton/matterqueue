## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — Foldex

**One Phrase** — *Fold the World* *(uncertain)*

**Core Sentence** — Make any paper folding tradition accessible to anyone through AI-generated templates, photo-to-fold matching, and a living library that grows through use

**One Paragraph** — Origami looks accessible and isn't — the diagrams are cryptic and most folding traditions are poorly documented. Foldex makes any tradition learnable: point your phone at something, get a fold, and follow plain-language steps with a printable template. The library compounds with every user and AI-generated pattern, and the cultural-preservation angle gives it gravity beyond a fun app. Paper airplanes get you in the door; the world's folding traditions are the ceiling.

**Key Insights**
- Photo input is the unlock — point at anything, get a fold
- Library compounds over time: AI synthesis plus community contribution
- Plain-language instructions plus printed fold-line templates solve origami's longstanding accessibility problem
- Cultural preservation angle — Vietnamese, Chinese, Japanese and other traditions poorly documented in accessible form
- Paper airplanes are an entry point and marketing hook, not the ceiling
- Open source framing removes monetization pressure and invites community contribution

**User** — Curious adults, kids with engaged parents, educators, cultural institutions, origami hobbyists, makers

**Model** — Open source core; potential premium surfaces (print subscriptions, educator licensing, cultural institution partnerships)

**Why It's Real**
- Origami accessibility gap is real and underserved
- Living library model means it gets more valuable with every user
- Cultural preservation gives it gravity beyond fun app
- Physical output completes the loop

**Flags** — Needs a strong maintainer vision to avoid scope sprawl; cultural accuracy requires community stewardship not just AI generation; folded object AR layer moved to Foldex from Patterntopia Yes-and

**Synergy**
- Patterntopia (shared World Surfaces instinct and physical output)
- Formcap (shared photo-to-object pipeline)
- Flowscape (fold sequences visualized in 3D)
