## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — DiffMutant

**One Phrase** — [FILL: one phrase — the sharpest distillation]

**Core Sentence** — A pattern matching engine that detects drift and change across streams and documents — surfacing what has shifted, what has diverged, and what patterns are emerging across any corpus over time

**One Paragraph** — Diff is a programmer's idea — show me what changed between two versions — and almost nothing applies it to the things that matter most: documents, relationships, attention, and behavior drifting over time. DiffMutant is a pattern-matching engine that detects that drift across any corpus, surfacing what has shifted, diverged, or quietly started becoming something else. It lives inside The Current as its drift-detection layer and shows up concretely as regime-change detection in HypothesisEngine. Build it once the corpus is rich enough to make 'what changed' worth knowing.

**Key Insights**
- Diff is a software concept — comparing two versions of a document to show what changed; DiffMutant extends this to streams, corpora, and behavioral patterns over time
- Drift detection is the valuable application — when does a document, conversation, relationship, or system start becoming something different from what it was
- Pattern matching across streams — finding structural similarities between different documents, conversations, or data streams that aren't obviously related
- HypothesisEngine is DiffMutant's primary concrete expression — the adapter layer architecture is how DiffMutant should be built across all domains; regime change detection is DiffMutant applied to price series
