## Idea Capsule — 2026-06-16

**Name** — EarlyRead

**One Phrase** — *Catch It Early* *(uncertain)*

**Core Sentence** — Give parents, teachers, and local learning centers a democratized triage tool that identifies students who show enough dyslexia and learning difference indicators to warrant professional assessment — closing the gap between suspicion and action that costs children years

**One Paragraph** — A fifth of kids have a learning difference, and the ones with persistent, educated parents get diagnosed while the rest get labeled lazy or disruptive — years lost to the gap between suspicion and action. EarlyRead is a triage tool, not a diagnosis: it identifies students showing enough dyslexia and learning-difference indicators to warrant professional assessment, then tells parents and teachers exactly what to do next, including their rights under IDEA and 504. Identification without a pathway is just anxiety with a label, so the pathway is the product. Public-health scale, grant-fundable, and built by someone who navigated the system firsthand.

**Key Insights**
- Not a diagnostic tool — a triage tool; "this student warrants professional assessment" is the entire output and it's defensible
- 20% of students have a learning issue — this is public health infrastructure not edtech niche
- Equity is the core argument — kids with persistent educated parents get diagnosed; kids without that advocacy get labeled lazy or disruptive
- Dyslexia and ADHD co-occur at high rates and mask each other — classifier needs to handle comorbidity patterns
- Identification without pathway is just anxiety with a label — tool must output next steps, rights under IDEA and 504, who to call
- Personal experience — you navigated this system with your own kids

**User** — Parents who suspect something but don't know what to do; classroom teachers who observe patterns; local learning centers; school districts, pediatricians

**Model** — Nonprofit or social venture; grant funding (NIH, Department of Education, learning disability foundations); coalition of local and national partner organizations; no monetization pressure

**Why It's Real**
- You've lived the system failure firsthand
- 20% prevalence makes this a public health argument — strong grant narrative
- Classifier approach well-suited to your data science background
- Local center starting point (Blosser) gives you a pilot environment close enough to observe

**Flags** — Must stay clearly in triage lane — any drift toward diagnosis creates legal and ethical exposure; training data quality and consent are critical; Shaywitz Yale work needs thorough review before building; clinical validation essential

**Synergy**
- DataMutants (data science workflow for learning indicator data)
- Syllably (learning difference accommodations and UDL connect)
- Phase Next (parents of kids with learning differences are often 50-something)
- Groundtruth (shared data governance model)
